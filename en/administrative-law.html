Administrative Law

Legal advice on administrative procedures, licences, authorisations and relations with public authorities in Portugal.

We assist with administrative law matters involving public authorities, administrative procedures, licences, authorisations, administrative acts and legal situations in which the exercise of a right or the development of an activity depends on public decision-making.

This area is particularly relevant where the client must interact with administrative bodies, challenge an administrative decision, regularise a situation or ensure that a project or activity is framed within the applicable public law requirements.

What We Handle

Administrative procedures and dealings with public authorities
Licences, permits and administrative authorisations
Administrative acts and their legal effects
Requests, objections and challenges in administrative matters
Urban planning and administrative constraints on property and projects
Administrative disputes and protection of legal positions

Administrative Procedures and Public Authorities

We provide legal advice on matters requiring interaction with public entities, including the submission of requests, responses to administrative notifications, clarification of requirements and legal framing of the procedure underway.

In many situations, the decisive issue lies in understanding exactly which procedural step is at stake, what powers the authority is exercising and how the client’s position should be legally presented and protected.

Licences, Authorisations and Administrative Acts

We assist in matters involving licences, permits, authorisations and other administrative acts that condition the exercise of rights, the use of assets or the implementation of projects and activities.

These situations often require careful reading of the applicable legal framework, the content of the administrative decision and the practical implications of compliance, refusal, limitation or delay by the competent authority.

Administrative Constraints and Protection of Rights

Administrative law frequently affects property, business activity, licensing, urban planning, mobility and the practical ability to carry out a project in Portugal.

For that reason, legal support in this area should combine technical rigour, documentary analysis and a clear strategy for safeguarding the client’s legal position in the face of administrative acts or omissions.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

Administrative law frequently intersects with investment, real estate and urban planning, public procurement, residence in Portugal and litigation and arbitration, depending on the nature of the procedure and the public authority involved.

If you require legal support on administrative procedures, licences, authorisations, administrative acts or relations with public authorities in Portugal, you may contact MG&A.
