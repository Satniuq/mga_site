Criminal Law

Legal assistance in situations with criminal relevance, with prompt, technically rigorous and discreet intervention at all stages of criminal proceedings.

We assist clients in criminal law matters, including situations involving complaints, criminal reports, constitution as a suspect, questioning, coercive measures, indictment, pre-trial stage and trial.

This area requires rapid response, legal rigour and particular care in defining the position to be taken from the outset, bearing in mind the sensitivity and consequences inherent in criminal proceedings.

What We Handle

Complaints, criminal reports and initial legal framing of the situation
Constitution as a suspect and legal assistance in questioning
Coercive measures and procedural response
Assistance during investigation, pre-trial stage and trial
Criminal defence and protection of the client’s procedural position
Assistance to injured parties and intervention in criminal proceedings

Initial Stage and Definition of Position

In criminal law, the earliest moments are often decisive. The way the situation is framed, the available documentation, the explanations given and the initial response may have a significant impact on the later development of the proceedings.

We provide legal support from this initial stage onwards, including analysis of the situation, definition of the response strategy and assistance in urgent or particularly sensitive procedural acts.

Investigation, Pre-Trial Stage and Trial

We assist the client throughout the different stages of criminal proceedings, including investigation, pre-trial stage and trial, ensuring legally consistent intervention suited to the facts at issue.

This work requires rigorous analysis of the procedural elements, attention to the available evidence and protection of the client’s procedural position at each stage of the proceedings.

Criminal Defence and Assistance to Injured Parties

We provide support both in the defence of those targeted in criminal proceedings and in the assistance of those who have been injured by facts with criminal relevance.

In either case, the aim is to ensure clear legal framing, appropriate procedural action and effective protection of the interests at stake.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

Criminal law may intersect with employment and labour law, corporate and commercial law and litigation and arbitration, whenever the criminal matter arises from broader professional, business or asset-related conflicts.

If you require legal support in criminal proceedings, criminal defence, questioning, coercive measures or assistance as an injured party, you may contact MG&A.
