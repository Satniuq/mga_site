D2 Visa in Portugal

Legal advice for entrepreneurs and self-employed professionals who intend to apply for the D2 Visa and organise their relocation to Portugal.

We assist with matters relating to the D2 Visa in Portugal, generally aimed at foreign nationals who intend to carry out self-employed activity or implement a business project with a view to residing in the country.

In many cases, the process requires more than the visa application in the strict sense: document preparation, business plan support, tax number, tax representation, bank account, possible company incorporation, coordination with an accountant and the practical organisation of relocation to Portugal.

Entrepreneurship, Self-Employed Activity and Project Structure

Depending on the specific case, the D2 Visa may be suitable for those who intend to open a business in Portugal, establish a company, carry out their own economic activity or practise a profession on a self-employed basis.

The key point is not an abstract formula of entrepreneurship, but the consistency of the project: the intended activity, its viability, the available means, the practical organisation of the move and the coherence between the applicant, the documentation and the plan submitted.

Documents, Business Plan and Application Preparation

The strength of a D2 process often depends on how the project is documented. In many cases, relevant elements include the business plan, proof of financial means, documents relating to the company or self-employed activity, bank account arrangements, accommodation solution and the personal documents required by the competent consular post.

It is also important to ensure the proper preparation of foreign documents, including legalisations, apostilles, translations and certifications where required, as well as consistency between what is prepared in Portugal and what is submitted in the consular process.

Company Structure, Relocation to Portugal and Continuity of Residence

In some cases, the project involves incorporating a company in Portugal; in others, the most appropriate solution will be self-employed activity. That choice depends on the nature of the business, the way the activity will be carried out, the existence of partners and the practical structure of the client’s presence in the country.

The D2 process also intersects with essential aspects of relocation to Portugal, such as tax organisation, accounting framework, property rental or acquisition, documentary regularisation and possible coordination with family matters and subsequent residence arrangements.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

The D2 Visa in Portugal frequently intersects with residence in Portugal, corporate and commercial law, investment, real estate and urban planning, notarial services and formal acts and family reunification, depending on the structure of the project and the way the client’s relocation is organised.
