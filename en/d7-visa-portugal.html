D7 Visa in Portugal

Legal advice for pensioners and holders of passive income who intend to reside in Portugal on the basis of stable and independent means of subsistence.

We assist with matters relating to the D7 Visa in Portugal, generally sought by foreign nationals who intend to live in the country on the basis of pensions, rental income, dividends, interest, royalties or other stable own means.

In many cases, the process requires more than the visa application in the strict sense: obtaining a tax number, possible tax representation, bank account arrangements, organisation of proof of income, accommodation in Portugal, document preparation and coordination between Portugal and the applicant’s country of residence.

Passive Income and the D7 Framework

The D7 Visa is particularly relevant for those who intend to reside in Portugal without basing the process on an entrepreneurial project or on professional activity to be carried out in the country, relying instead on passive income or other stable own means.

The central issue is not merely having assets, but being able to demonstrate, in a coherent and properly documented way, the origin, regularity and sufficiency of the income that will support an effective residence in Portugal.

Documentation, Means of Subsistence and Application Preparation

The strength of a D7 process often depends on how the financial and documentary situation is prepared. Particular importance is usually attached to proof of income, bank documentation, personal documents, criminal record certificate, accommodation in Portugal and the specific requirements of the competent consular post.

It is also important to ensure the proper preparation of foreign documents, including legalisations, apostilles, translations and certifications where required, as well as consistency between the elements handled in Portugal and the documents submitted in the consular application.

Relocation to Portugal and Continuity of Residence

The D7 is intended for those who plan to live in Portugal in a real and stable manner. For that reason, the process often intersects with decisions concerning housing, organisation of daily life, possible family reunification, assets held in other jurisdictions and preparation of an effective presence in the country.

In many cases, residence should not be treated in isolation. The stability of the solution also depends on the way in which the applicant’s personal, family, asset-related and documentary circumstances are coordinated.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

The D7 Visa in Portugal frequently intersects with residence in Portugal, investment, real estate and urban planning, family reunification, family, succession and estate planning and notarial services and formal acts, depending on how the client’s relocation to Portugal is organised.
