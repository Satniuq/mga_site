Employment and Labour Law

Legal advice on labour relations, the organisation of the employment relationship and the management of conflict between employers and employees.

We assist with employment and labour law matters relating to recruitment, employment contracts, the performance of the employment relationship, the parties’ duties, disciplinary procedures, termination of employment and labour disputes.

This area is particularly relevant where it is necessary to structure the employment relationship correctly, prevent legal risks or respond to situations of breach, conflict or breakdown of the employment bond.

What We Handle

Recruitment and employment contracts
Rights and duties of employers and employees
Changes to working conditions and internal labour organisation
Disciplinary procedures and employer reaction to misconduct
Termination of employment and dismissal
Labour disputes and protection of the parties’ position

Employment Contracts and Organisation of Labour Relations

We provide legal advice on the preparation, review and interpretation of employment contracts, as well as on the legal framework governing the employment relationship throughout its duration.

In many cases, the key point lies in ensuring that the contractual structure, the duties of the parties and the day-to-day organisation of work are legally sound and suited to the specific context of the employer and the employee.

Disciplinary Procedures, Breach and Dismissal

We assist in situations involving disciplinary issues, breach of labour duties, dismissal and other forms of termination of employment, with attention to the procedural framework and the protection of the client’s legal position.

These matters require rigorous assessment of the facts, applicable deadlines, available documentation and the legal grounds relied upon, whether from the employer’s or the employee’s perspective.

Labour Conflict and Protection of Interests

Labour relations may give rise to disputes involving pay, duties, workplace conduct, termination of the contract or disagreement over the parties’ rights and obligations.

In these situations, legal support should combine technical rigour, clear framing of the employment relationship and the most appropriate legal response for the protection of the interests at stake.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

Employment and labour law frequently intersects with corporate and commercial law, criminal law and litigation and arbitration, particularly where the labour matter arises within a broader business, disciplinary or contentious context.

If you require legal support on employment contracts, disciplinary procedures, dismissal, termination of employment or labour disputes, you may contact MG&A.
