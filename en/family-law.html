Family Law

Legal advice on family matters with personal, patrimonial and parental impact in Portugal.

We assist with family law matters relating to divorce, separation, parental responsibilities, maintenance, de facto relationships and the legal organisation of family relationships.

This area requires sensitivity, clarity and technical rigour, particularly where children, patrimonial balance and decisions with lasting impact on personal and family life are involved.

What We Handle

Divorce and separation
Regulation of parental responsibilities
Changes to parental responsibility arrangements
Child maintenance and other financial support due to children
De facto relationships and the patrimonial effects of life in common
Patrimonial matters connected with the end of the relationship

Divorce, Separation and Family Reorganisation

We provide legal advice in situations involving the breakdown of family life, helping to frame the divorce or separation and the issues that arise from it within the appropriate legal context.

In these situations, it is important to define clearly the personal and patrimonial effects of the end of the relationship, preventing unnecessary conflict and ensuring a stable legal basis for the reorganisation of family life.

Parental Responsibilities and Maintenance

We assist with matters concerning children, including regulation of parental responsibilities, residence, contact arrangements, the exercise of parental responsibilities and maintenance.

The aim is to find legally sound solutions suited to the specific context, with particular attention to the best interests of the child and the need for stability in family life.

De Facto Relationships and Patrimonial Issues

We also provide legal support in matters connected with de facto relationships and patrimonial issues that may arise within family relationships, including ownership of assets, contributions to common life and the legal effects of the end of the relationship.

These situations often require a careful analysis of the facts, the available documentation and the legal framework applicable to each case.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

Family law frequently intersects with succession and inheritance, civil law, notarial services and formal acts and litigation and arbitration, depending on the nature of the specific matter.

If you require support with divorce, separation, parental responsibilities, maintenance or other family law matters, you may contact MG&A.
