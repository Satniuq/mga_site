Family Reunification in Portugal

Legal advice on residence for spouses, partners, children and other eligible family members, with attention to proof of family links, documentation and the family’s relocation to Portugal.

We assist with family reunification matters in Portugal, in situations where a person already residing in the country wishes to bring certain family members to join them within the appropriate legal and documentary framework.

In many cases, the process requires more than the submission of the application itself: proof of the family relationship, verification of the principal family member’s residence status, foreign documents, legalisations, translations, proof of accommodation and coordination between Portugal and the country where the family members are located.

Eligible Family Members and Household Structure

Family reunification commonly concerns a spouse, partner, minor children and, in certain cases, dependent adult children or other family members whose dependency has legal relevance. The analysis must always be carried out on a case-by-case basis.

The essential point is not merely to identify the family relationship, but to determine whether the specific household structure and the available documentation make it possible to support the application consistently and without avoidable weaknesses.

Proof of Family Links and Documentary Preparation

In many applications, the strength of the process depends on how the family relationship is documented. Certificates, identity documents, proof of the principal family member’s residence, evidence relating to accommodation in Portugal and other documents tailored to the particular case are often of central importance.

It is also important to ensure the proper preparation of foreign documents, including legalisations, apostilles, translations and certifications where required, as well as consistency between the elements handled in Portugal and the documents obtained abroad.

Minors, Consents and Relocation to Portugal

Where minor children, parental responsibilities, custody, consent of the other parent or authorisations for relocation are involved, family reunification requires a particularly careful analysis of the family reality and of the supporting documentation.

The process also intersects with the family’s effective relocation to Portugal, including housing, household organisation and the stability of the principal family member’s residence solution, and should therefore not be treated in isolation.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

Family reunification in Portugal frequently intersects with residence in Portugal, family law, notarial services and formal acts, succession and inheritance and investment, real estate and planning, depending on the composition of the household and the way the family’s relocation is organised.
