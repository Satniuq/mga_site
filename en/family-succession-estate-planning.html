Family, Succession and Estate Planning

Legal advice on wills, inheritance, estate division and the legal organisation of family assets in Portugal.

We assist with succession matters involving inheritance, wills, estate division, gifts and the organisation of family assets, both in planning situations and in matters arising after death.

This area is particularly relevant where it is necessary to protect assets, anticipate future transmission, clarify the position of heirs or organise an inheritance with legal certainty and family stability.

What We Handle

Wills and succession planning
Inheritance and identification of heirs
Acceptance or renunciation of inheritance
Estate division and partition of assets
Gifts and inter vivos transfers of assets
Family asset organisation across generations

Wills and Estate Planning

We provide legal advice on succession planning, including the preparation of wills and the legal organisation of asset transmission in a way that is coherent with the client’s family structure, assets and long-term intentions.

Good planning can reduce future uncertainty, prevent disputes between heirs and ensure that the transmission of assets takes place with greater clarity, legal security and practical continuity.

Inheritance, Heirs and Estate Division

We assist with matters arising after death, including the identification of heirs, the legal framing of the inheritance, acceptance or renunciation and the steps needed for estate division and partition of assets.

In these cases, it is often necessary to combine legal analysis, documentary review and practical coordination between family members, assets and formal requirements connected with the succession process.

Gifts, Family Assets and Intergenerational Organisation

We also provide legal support on gifts and other asset transfers within the family, as well as on broader estate planning decisions intended to organise the preservation and transmission of family assets across generations.

These matters often require careful attention to the legal effects of the act, the position of family members and the impact of the chosen solution on the future balance of the estate.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

This area frequently intersects with family law, civil law, investment, real estate and urban planning, notarial services and formal acts and litigation and arbitration, depending on the assets involved and the family structure of the matter.

If you require legal support on wills, inheritance, estate division, gifts or family estate planning in Portugal, you may contact MGA.
