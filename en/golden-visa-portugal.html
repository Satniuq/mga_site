Investment and Residence by Investment

The Golden Visa regime is relevant for foreign nationals who intend to obtain residence in Portugal through an investment route that fits within the applicable legal framework.

In this context, it is essential to analyse not only whether the intended investment is legally suitable, but also whether the overall structure is coherent from the point of view of documentation, timing, source of funds, family composition and the client’s broader objectives in Portugal.

Documentation, Structuring and Application Preparation

Golden Visa matters usually require careful preparation of both the investment side and the residence side of the process. This may include personal documentation, criminal record certificates, proof of investment, banking arrangements, tax number, declarations, foreign documents and coordination with the relevant supporting entities.

It is also important to ensure the proper preparation of foreign documents, including legalisations, apostilles, translations and certifications where required, as well as consistency between the investment structure and the documents used for the residence application.

Family, Assets and Long-Term Organisation

In many cases, residence by investment is not an isolated matter. It is connected with family planning, protection of assets, succession considerations, acquisition or rental of property, future relocation and the way in which the client intends to establish a stable legal presence in Portugal.

For that reason, legal advice in this area should take into account not only the immediate application, but also the continuity and coherence of the client’s personal, family and asset structure over time.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

The Golden Visa in Portugal frequently intersects with residence in Portugal, investment, real estate and urban planning, corporate and commercial law, family reunification, family, succession and estate planning and notarial services and formal acts, depending on the investment model and the way the client’s presence in Portugal is organised.
