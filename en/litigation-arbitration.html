Dispute Resolution and Pre-Litigation Stage

Not every conflict begins in court. In many cases, the decisive stage lies in the initial analysis of the situation, the review of the existing documentation, the clarification of the client’s legal position and the assessment of the most appropriate course of action.

We provide support at this pre-litigation stage, including legal framing of the dispute, preparation of formal communications, negotiation and attempts to achieve settlement, whenever that is appropriate to the protection of the interests at stake.

Judicial Proceedings, Arbitration and Enforcement

Where the conflict requires procedural action, we assist in judicial and arbitral proceedings across different types of dispute, as well as in enforcement situations, compulsory recovery of debts and damages claims.

Intervention at this stage requires a legally consistent strategy based on the analysis of the facts, the available evidence, the procedural risks and the client’s concrete objectives.

Asset-Related, Personal and Business Conflicts

Litigation may arise in very different contexts, from asset-related and contractual disputes between private parties to family, employment, condominium, commercial or corporate conflicts.

The nature of the dispute varies from case to case and requires a legal response that is tailored to the facts, the subject matter involved and the interests to be protected.

Connection with Other Practice Areas

Litigation and arbitration frequently intersect with civil law, family law, employment and labour law, corporate and commercial law, administrative law and public procurement, depending on the subject matter from which the dispute arises.

If you require legal support in judicial disputes, arbitration, debt recovery, enforcement, damages claims or the definition of a litigation strategy, you may contact MGA.
